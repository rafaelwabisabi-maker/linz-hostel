# Call Script — Daniel × Rafael
**Date:** Feb 26, 2026 | **Record the call.**

---

## Opening (30 sec)
> "Hey Dani, glad we could connect. How's the throat? Hope you're feeling better.
> I put together a quick summary of where I think we are — want to run through it fast so we leave this call with everything clear?"

---

## BLOCK 1 — Confirm the role (2 min)
**Say:**
> "You mentioned you want me on the team — love that. Before I go deeper, I just want to make sure we're on the same page on what that means for both of us."

**Ask:**
> "The written reference I have is Friedrich's contract at €1,250/month. You mentioned €3k verbally — can we confirm what my number actually is?"

**Listen for:** a specific number. If vague → "Can we just drop a message after this call confirming it? Even WhatsApp is fine."

---

## BLOCK 2 — After activation (2 min)
**Say:**
> "I want to set this up so it works for both of us long term. Once funding comes in — what does full engagement look like? How many hours a week are you thinking, and what are the top deliverables you'd hold me to?"

**Listen for:** scope, hours, any mention of scaling as the project grows.

> If he asks what you expect → "I'd say 20-25h/week once we're operational, focused on tech infrastructure, content systems, and digital tools. If the project replicates to other cities, I'd want that reflected in how we grow together."

---

## BLOCK 3 — Pre-funding phase (1 min)
**Say:**
> "I'm happy to keep building in the pre-funding phase — that's the nature of early stage. One thing I'd love to explore: can this be a two-way street? Like, if I need your network or a conversation with someone in your world for my own projects, could I count on that? Not a formal thing — just good partnership energy."

**Listen for:** yes/warm response. Don't push if vague — just plant the seed.

---

## BLOCK 4 — Future-proofing (1 min)
**Say:**
> "Last thing on the agreement side — we're both building in a world where AI is replacing a lot of tasks fast. My value here is judgment and strategy, not just execution. I'd love a shared understanding that as long as I'm delivering, I have a stable spot on this team. Does that feel fair to you?"

**Listen for:** verbal yes. That's enough — you have a recording.

---

## BLOCK 5 — How you work (1 min)
> "Dani, one thing about how I work best — I have ADHD too, so scattered communication across multiple people really kills my momentum. What works great for me is a direct line with you: you give me the direction, I build and deliver. Way faster and cleaner. I'm happy to connect with the team when it makes sense, but as a default — just you and me. Is that okay?"

---

## BLOCK 6 — Quick decisions needed (2 min)

**Pitch app:**
> "I have the interactive pitch website built and ready to go live — takes 10 minutes to deploy. I just need your eyes on it first. Can we do a quick screen share now, or should I send you the local preview link and you give me feedback by end of day?"

**Video:**
> "Script and shot plan are ready. You answered your own brief in the voice memo — 30 seconds, investors, flashy cuts, pitch deck visual style. I just need a 'go' from you and I'll start production."

**Notion:**
> "Can you send me the Notion invite directly? Just need that access to stay in sync."

---

## Closing (30 sec)
> "Perfect. So after this call: you drop me a WhatsApp confirming the compensation, you send the Notion invite, and I'll send you the pitch app preview link for your feedback. Sound good?"

## What to listen for / red flags
- If he's vague on the number → get a WhatsApp confirmation, don't leave the call without it
- If he deflects on the scope → that's a signal to cap your hours explicitly in the agreement
- If he mentions Friedo for anything → "Happy for you to align with Friedrich on your end, I'll work from your direction"
