## THE FORMULA (How The Social Hub Works)

The Social Hub video is a **montage of life inside the space** — not a brand story, not cinematic
atmosphere. It shows: people arriving, playing ping pong, cooking together, coworking, laughing in
the lounge, swimming, discovering unexpected moments. Fast cuts for gen Z. Every single shot moves.

**For KraftWerk investors: same shots, held 6–8 seconds instead of 2–3. Let them absorb.**

The emotional message is not told — it's shown. You watch 60 seconds of life at The Social Hub
and you FEEL the answer to "what is this place?" before any words explain it.

## THE CAMERA RULE (Most Important)

**The Social Hub has ZERO static shots.** Every frame moves. This is what creates energy.

```
Shot types used in The Social Hub — use all of these:
- Tracking shot (following a person walking through space)
- Overhead/top-down (food, table, communal moments)
- Low angle looking up (making spaces feel grand)
- First-person POV (entering a room, going through a door)
- Crash zoom (comedic/energetic beat)
- Whip pan (fast blur transition between spaces)
- Drone dive (exterior approach)
- Fisheye (corridors, unexpected perspective)
- Underwater equivalent (we don't have a pool — replace with looking up through a skylight)
```

**For investors:** All the same shot types, but execute at 60% speed. Hold each shot 6–8 sec.

## MUSIC DIRECTION

The Social Hub: upbeat, pop-influenced, high energy.
KraftWerk investor version: same FEEL, lower tempo, more sophisticated.

```
Gemini Lyria 3 prompt:
"Warm indie electronic, subtle groove, 80 BPM, positive but not hyperactive,
piano melody over gentle synth pad, light percussion, instrumental only,
builds slightly from 0:00 to 0:30 then settles into steady warmth,
European boutique hotel vibes, 120 seconds"
```

**Levels:**
- Opening/problem (0:00–0:14): 30% under narration, slightly hushed
- Vision/life montage (0:14–0:58): 25% — music present, warm
- Numbers (0:58–1:10): 15% — near silence, let the data land
- Ask/Daniel (1:10–1:35): OFF during Daniel speaking
- End card: 60% — music swells briefly, then fades
