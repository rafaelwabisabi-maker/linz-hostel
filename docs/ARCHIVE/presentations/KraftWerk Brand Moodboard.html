04 — Illustration Library

The Complete Visual Language

Daniel's decks contain a rich, consistent hand-drawn illustration library across 5 presentations. These are the core brand visuals — every future asset should match this style.

Illustration Characteristics
  • Line Work
    Black/dark outlines with hand-drawn quality, not perfectly geometric
  • Color Fills
    Warm earth tones — peach, cream, soft orange, muted brown — matching the brand palette
  • Subjects
    Diverse groups in communal settings, cozy interiors, plants, bookshelves, industrial ceilings, makers at work
  • Linz Imagery
    Historic cityscape + Danube views used alongside illustrations (Linz church tower, Tabakfabrik, golden sunset)
  • Special Formats
    Superhero-style founder portraits, before/after renderings, circular journey diagrams, celebration scenes
Mood & Feeling
"Warm, grounded, inclusive — like a sketchbook from someone who genuinely cares about people."

The illustration style communicates handmade authenticity, human warmth, and unpretentiousness. It avoids corporate polish while maintaining professionalism. This is the visual embodiment of the "engineered connection" concept — intentional design that still feels organic and personal.

RECOMMENDATION
Commission the same illustrator (or find the style source) for all future brand materials — website, merch, signage, social media. This style IS the brand.

Key Architectural Features to Preserve in Design

  • Exposed concrete columns with raw aggregate texture — these become the "industrial soul" of the space
  • Factory grid windows with teal/green steel frames — unique to Tabakfabrik, a signature visual element
  • Terracotta/red brick floor tiles — warm, historical, grounding (connect to brand terracotta color)
  • High ceilings with exposed HVAC ductwork — keep industrial, don't hide it
  • Natural light patterns through grid windows — the diamond light patterns on floors are beautiful
  • Orange/rust steel structural elements — patina tells the building's story

KraftWerk Fundraising Takeaways

  • Equity crowdfunding works for hospitality — Mirror Hotels proved you can overfund 344% with strong brand + story
  • Community co-ops build loyalty — Kosmos and Hub.House show member-owners become lifelong ambassadors
  • Reward-based perks drive early traction — Oculis sold future stays; KraftWerk could offer founding member hostel nights
  • Start small, scale later — BrewDog began with one bar and grew to £75M+ across 6 rounds
  • Community will pay to save what matters — Sun and Co.'s €17k rescue proves emotional attachment converts to capital
  • KraftWerk's €400k target is realistic — well within the range these projects have demonstrated
