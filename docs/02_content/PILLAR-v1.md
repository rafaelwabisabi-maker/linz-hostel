# We're not building a hostel. We're building Linz's missing connective tissue.

Austria ranks 49th out of 53 countries for expat friendliness.

Not because people aren't welcome. Not because the city lacks culture or jobs or good universities.

Because there is nowhere for newcomers to plug in.

Linz welcomes over a million overnight visitors every year. Twenty-four thousand international students arrive at JKU. Hundreds of researchers, artists, and performers show up for Ars Electronica each September. Tens of thousands more come for Pflasterspektakel, Urfahraner Markt, and the city's growing startup scene.

They come. They experience. They leave.

Without a single local connection. Without knowing the researcher who works three streets away. Without meeting the startup founder who needed exactly the skill they have.

The city has everything. It just lacks the room where everything meets.

---

## The room we're building

KraftWerk is a 90-bed hostel at TabakFabrik Linz — the repurposed tobacco factory that has become the city's creative hub.

But "hostel" is the wrong word.

A hostel gives you a bed, a locker, and a Wi-Fi password.

What we're building gives you the city.

The model is called the Anchor. The core idea: design every element of the space — the lobby, the lounge, the lunch counter, the communal table — around engineered collision. Not forced networking. Not awkward icebreakers. Just a building that keeps introducing you to people you were going to need anyway.

The mural at the entrance isn't decoration. It's a map of every institution, university, and creative organisation connected to this space.

JKU students see their university's logo on day one. Ars Electronica researchers see their festival listed alongside their host organisation. Volkshilfe staff members see their programme named on the wall.

They're not staying in a hostel. They're staying inside a coalition.

---

## How we built the coalition

The Anchor Model only works if the partnerships are real. We've spent the past year making them real.

**JKU International** is the first pillar. Linz's flagship university sends thousands of international students and researchers through the city every semester. KraftWerk is the only venue built specifically to connect them to the local ecosystem on arrival — not after weeks of navigating the city alone.

**Ars Electronica** is the second. The world's most renowned media art festival doesn't just exhibit at KraftWerk during September. They run active research experiments in the space. The Circadian Lighting System in our lounge is a live prototype — shifting to amber at 10am as part of a study on focus and wellbeing. Our sleep pods will be equipped with Sonic Pillows, another Ars Electronica research track. Guests don't visit the festival and come back to a hostel. They sleep inside the lab.

**Volkshilfe and AMS** anchor the social mission. Our staffing model integrates a workforce re-entry programme: local residents rebuilding their professional lives through real hospitality roles. When you order lunch at the Fresh Counter, the person serving you may have been unemployed three months ago. By the time you leave, your stay has contributed to someone's second chance. That's not a marketing line. It's the operating model.

**Factory 300** — TabakFabrik's own startup ecosystem — fills the evening table. Our Founder's Table is a nightly informal gathering in the lobby. The kind of conversation that happens over dinner between a JKU researcher, a Factory 300 founder, and an Ars Electronica exhibitor is the kind that turns into a collaboration six months later.

The mural at the entrance shows who's connected to this building. The Founder's Table is where they actually meet.

---

## The season that proves the model

The real test comes three times a year.

In September, 100,000 people descend on Linz for Ars Electronica. Most experience it from the street. Our guests experience it from inside: the facade runs live LED data-visualisations from the exhibitors. The sleep pods are equipped with experimental audio prototypes from the festival's research track. For one week, KraftWerk is the festival's experimental satellite — the only venue in Linz where you don't just attend Ars Electronica, you sleep inside it.

In July, Pflasterspektakel brings 200,000 people into the streets. The international performers stay with us. They rehearse in the courtyard, share breakfast with guests, and go on stage to audiences who already know them. The hostel is the backstage. The lobby is the green room. The city is the stage.

In May and September, the Urfahraner Markt lights up across the Danube. The Ferris wheel, the traditional food stalls, the multigenerational crowds. We close the gap. Guests come back from the fair to a bar serving local specialties sourced from partner farms, explained by someone who grew up in Upper Austria. Tech Linz and Traditional Linz sharing a counter.

Three events. Three coalition moments. Zero coincidence.

## The numbers

The model works financially because it serves multiple revenue streams simultaneously.

**Base occupancy:** 90 beds across private rooms, dorms, and premium singles. Conservative baseline projection: 38% occupancy in Year 1. Break-even at Month 14. By Year 2: EBITDA +€65k. Year 3: +€140k.

**Funding structure:**
- €100k from investors (stille Beteiligung, minimum €10,000 ticket, 5-year buyback or profit share)
- €220k Sparkasse bank loan (in application)
- €80k from AWS Creative Impact and OÖ Tourismus grants (in application)
- Total: €400,000

We're not asking anyone to believe in an idea. We're asking them to join a coalition that's already forming.
