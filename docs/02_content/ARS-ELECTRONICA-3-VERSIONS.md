## VERSION A: "EMOTIONAL HOOK" — Lead with Feeling, Close with Numbers

**Philosophy:** Every scene opens with a human moment. The viewer FEELS before they THINK. Numbers only appear in scenes 9-10. This is the "documentary filmmaker" approach.

**Best for:** Audiences who need to fall in love with the concept before seeing the deal.

## VERSION B: "DATA PUNCH" — Numbers First, Emotion as Proof

**Philosophy:** Open with the market reality. Hit hard with data in the first 3 scenes. Then show the HUMAN proof of why the numbers work. This is the "investor pitch" approach.

**Best for:** Finance people, politicians, grant committees. People who need the business case FIRST.

## VERSION C: "CINEMATIC STORYTELLER" — Pure Visual Cinema, Minimal Text

**Philosophy:** Trust the visuals. Voiceover is sparse and poetic. On-screen text almost nonexistent. The video speaks through images and rhythm. This is the "film festival trailer" approach.

**Best for:** Creative audiences, festival organizers, cultural institutions. People who respond to atmosphere and vision.

## RECOMMENDATION

**For Ars Electronica specifically:** Test Version C first. The audience (festival organizers) responds to cinematic vision, not spreadsheets. They already know their own numbers.

**For Sparkasse/investors:** Use Version B's approach. They need the business case upfront.

**For JKU:** Adapt Version A. The human story of a student arriving resonates with university partnerships.

**Workflow:** Test all 3 in vids.new. The one that generates the best stock footage matches wins — because the visual prompts are the main differentiator in Vids output quality.

## HOW TO USE

1. Go to [vids.new](https://vids.new)
2. Click "Storyboard" (not "Help me create")
3. Copy the 3-column table from one version above
4. Paste into the prompt box
5. Vids generates the video timeline
6. Use Veo 3.1 sidebar to swap any clips that don't match
7. Adjust voiceover vs. music levels
8. Export as MP4

Repeat for each version. Compare outputs side by side.
