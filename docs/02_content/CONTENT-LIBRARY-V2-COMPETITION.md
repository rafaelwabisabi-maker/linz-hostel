We're combining what housing knows with what hostels know. And adding what neither has tried: a protocol that turns meeting into making.

**LI-02 — "The Gap in Hybrid Hospitality"**

Three models define hybrid hospitality right now. All three leave the same gap open.

**Selina** (130+ locations, 25 countries) combines beds with co-working and wellness. Community is an amenity. They don't measure whether guests connect.

**Common** (6,000+ units, US) proves community-oriented design commands rent premiums. But their residents are economically identical. Bonding capital. Not bridging.

**The Social Hub** (17 European cities) maximizes functional diversity per site. Hotel, student housing, co-working, events. No pedagogical program. No social outcome data.

The Linz Hostel occupies the gap all three leave open.

The commercial shell generates revenue. The Teachcation protocol generates data. The data generates grant eligibility. **The grant eligibility subsidizes the mission that commerce alone can't sustain.**

That's the flywheel. That's the moat.

We're not competing with these operators. We're doing the one thing none of them does: measuring whether belonging actually happens.

If you've built in hybrid hospitality or social infrastructure — I'd like to compare notes.
