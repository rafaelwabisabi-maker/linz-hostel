By converting a commercial shell into a 90-bed facility, we provide essential housing for the 200,000+ annual visitors to the area while establishing a "Social Anchor" for the local community.

### Conservative Performance Assumptions — Scenario A (Base Case)

> This baseline assumes **NO subsidies** and **NO social rent discounts**. It represents the "Worst Case" viability.

- **Average Daily Rate (ADR):** €32.00 (Market conservative; Competitors: €38–45)
- **Occupancy ramp-up:**
  - Year 1: 38% avg (Q1 25% → Q4 45%)
  - Year 2: 52% avg
  - Year 3: 65% avg (stabilised)
- **Break-even point:** Operating cash flow positive by Month 14
- **DSCR:** >1.3x by Year 2

### Risk Mitigation Strategy

1. **Revenue Floor:** 20% of inventory (18 beds) allocated to "Social Rental" (long-term contracts), guaranteeing base revenue regardless of tourism seasonality
2. **Flexible Cost Structure:** "Live-in" staffing model reduces cash payroll; cleaning outsourced to variable-cost partners
3. **Licensing Compliance:** Full WKO engagement for Gewerbeberechtigung and Betriebsanlagengenehmigung prior to launch
4. **Market research:** Hostels with "Vibe" show consistently high booking rates (Wild Elephant, Zostel case studies)

## PART 2: FULL DETAILED BUSINESS PLAN

### 1. Executive Summary

"Linz Hostel: The Social Impact Hub" is a 90-bed hospitality venture serving the growing "Budget & Business" sector in Linz. Located strategically close to the Tabakfabrik, it capitalises on the existing ecosystem of 3,000 employees and regular large-scale events that currently lack affordable accommodation in the immediate vicinity.

**Hybrid Model:**
1. **Commercial Hospitality** — High-volume short-stay tourism (70% revenue)
2. **Social Anchor** — Long-term social rental and community lounge (30% revenue/stability)

This plan demonstrates financial viability under conservative market conditions, with significant upside through public subsidies and strategic partnerships.

---

### 2. Market and Location

#### 2.1 The Linz Market

Linz recorded over 1 million overnight stays in 2024. The market is saturated with mid-range business hotels (€90+) but critically underserved in the modern budget segment (€30–50).

**Target Guests:**
- International students (JKU)
- Festival attendees (Ars Electronica, Pflasterspektakel)
- Digital Nomads
- Tabakfabrik workshop participants
- Backpackers

**Competitor Analysis:** Existing youth hostels are geographically distant from the creative centre or suffer from outdated facilities.

**USP:** Location + Community + Partnerships

#### 2.2 Primary Location — Adjacent to Tabakfabrik

Primary candidate: ~650m² commercial shell in close proximity to Tabakfabrik.

- **Strategic fit:** Easy access to "The Grand Garage" and Tabakfabrik event halls — capturing pull factor without high overhead of an interior unit
- **Fallback strategy:** Secondary locations in District 4020 suitable for "Split Concept" (sleeping unit separate from Social Lounge). Financing finalised only upon signing a concrete LOI.

---

### 3. Business Model and Services

#### 3.1 Accommodation (The Core)

- **Inventory:** 90 revenue beds
- **Format:** Pod-style privacy bunks — maximise density while ensuring guest comfort
- **Pricing:** Dynamic pricing model focusing on volume. Base rate: €32

#### 3.2 The Social Lounge & Co-working ("Flow Zone")

100m² multi-use space acting as "Living Room" for guests and locals. Monetised via daily passes for non-guests; included in room rate for residents.

#### 3.3 Food & Beverage

- **Model:** Limited service — high-margin automated vending (Grab & Go) + Coffee Bar
- **Reasoning:** Reduces staffing complexity and regulatory burden vs. full restaurant kitchen

---

### 4. Licensing and Legal Setup

Operating in Austria requires strict adherence to Gewerbeordnung (Trade Regulation Act).

**Required licences:**
1. **Hospitality (Gastgewerbe):** Required for accommodation and serving drinks
2. **Facility Permit (Betriebsanlagengenehmigung):** Required for the physical location (fire safety, noise, hygiene)

**Current status:** In consultation with WKO to verify specific Befähigungsnachweis (Certificate of Competence).

**Plan:** If founders do not hold specific credentials, a commercial manager (gewerberechtlicher Geschäftsführer) will be appointed or hired (20h/week) to satisfy legal requirements immediately upon GmbH founding.

> **Regulatory risk acknowledged:** Operating without a licence leads to immediate shutdown. No commercial activity will commence until the Gewerbeschein is issued.
