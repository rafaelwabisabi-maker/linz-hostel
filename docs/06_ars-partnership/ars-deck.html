The Research

Why Architecture Matters

01

Third Places

Oldenburg, 1989

Spaces that are neither home nor work — cafés, lounges, squares — are where communities actually form. 35+ years of peer-reviewed validation.

Our Flow Zone is designed as exactly this.

02

Weak Ties

Granovetter, 1973

The person who changes your career is rarely your best friend — it's the stranger you sat next to at breakfast.

Casual encounters produce more novel ideas. One of the most-cited findings in social science.

03

Serendipity by Design

MIT Building 20 · Pixar HQ

MIT Building 20: 9 Nobel laureates. Pixar HQ: one central atrium, maximum collision.

Proximity + shared space + no agenda = unexpected connection.

Context

More Than
Hospitality

Austria officially recognises social prescribing — connecting people to community spaces as a health intervention. Six federal states are running pilots, with an 86% success rate.

The Basecamp's model is designed around the same principle: shared spaces that reduce isolation and build connection for the young, mobile, international populations of the creative district.

Grant opportunities. Research partnerships. Real impact metrics.
