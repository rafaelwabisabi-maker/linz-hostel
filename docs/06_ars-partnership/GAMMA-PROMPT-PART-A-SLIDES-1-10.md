### SLIDE 9 — THE SCIENCE BEHIND IT
**Image prompt:** Abstract architectural interior with warm light streaming through geometric wooden patterns, creating beautiful shadows, museum-like atmosphere, contemplative and inviting
**Headline:** Why Architecture Matters for Connection
**Layout:** 3 research cards

**Card 1 — Third Places**
Spaces that are neither home nor work — cafés, lounges, public squares — are where communities actually form. (Oldenburg, 1989 — 35+ years of peer-reviewed validation.) Our Flow Zone is designed as exactly this.

**Card 2 — Weak Ties**
The person who changes your career is rarely your best friend — it's the stranger you sat next to at breakfast. Casual encounters produce more novel ideas and breakthroughs than strong ties. (Granovetter, 1973 — one of the most cited findings in social science.)

**Card 3 — Serendipity by Design**
MIT Building 20: 9 Nobel laureates. Pixar HQ: one central atrium, maximum collision. The principle is simple — proximity + shared space + no agenda = unexpected connection.
