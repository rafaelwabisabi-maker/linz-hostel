# LINZ HOSTEL x ARS ELECTRONICA — Video Scripts v3 (Grounded)
> **Purpose:** Partnership pitch video — convince Ars to take the meeting and consider pre-funding
> **Spine:** #5 Village — "Festivals have venues. Festivals don't have villages."
> **Voice:** Liam — Energetic, Social Media Creator (ElevenLabs). Same settings as V3.
> **Date:** 2026-03-14 (v3)
> **All facts:** Verified March 2026.
>
> **DESIGN PRINCIPLE:** This is a pitch to an institutional partner, not a brand film.
> The viewer should think: "This is well thought through. This solves a real problem.
> The numbers are reasonable. The risk is zero. I should take the meeting."
>
> **RULES:**
> - No superlatives or unverifiable claims
> - No grandiose "first in Europe" / "most important" language
> - No implicit accusation that Ars missed something or failed
> - No social science lectures (Granovetter stays out of the narration)
> - No manifesto about loneliness or the future of gathering
> - Practical information within the first 30 seconds
> - Respectful of the power dynamic — we are asking, they are deciding
> - No hostel taglines as closing lines
> - Ends with a clear, concrete next step

---

# TIMING REFERENCE
> Liam at 1.07x ≈ 2.1 words/sec in connected speech.
> Each "..." pause = 0.5 sec.
> Formula: (words / 2.1) + (pause_count × 0.5) = chapter duration
> 0.88x chapters: (words / 1.7) + (pause_count × 0.5)

# SCRIPT A — "THE PRACTICAL CASE"
## ~90 Seconds | 7 Chapters + Title + End Card
> **Approach:** Leads with what it is, what we're asking, and what Ars gets.
> Numbers early. Vision earned, not assumed.
> **Best for:** First send. The person receiving this has 2 minutes and wants to know
> if this is worth a meeting. Give them the answer in 30 seconds, then give them
> a reason to care for the next 60.

# RECOMMENDATION

**Script C is the strongest for Ars.**

It's 70 seconds. It answers every question. It hides nothing. The transparency — "It helps us demonstrate demand to Sparkasse" — is the most persuasive thing in any of the three scripts, because it treats the Ars decision-maker as an adult.
