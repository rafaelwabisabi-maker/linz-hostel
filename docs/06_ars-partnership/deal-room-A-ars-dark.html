The Full Story

12 movements. Read when you're ready.

MOVEMENT I
September. Linz. Sound artist from Tokyo. Biologist from Lagos. 19-year-old from Graz. By 10 PM, same kitchen. Nobody planned this. The building did.
MOVEMENT II
1979 Ars born. 2009 Linz European Capital of Culture, 3.5 million attendances. 2026: 122,000 people come, sleep scattered across fifty hotels, eat alone. Stages exist. Excellent ones. Village doesn't.
MOVEMENT III
You programme every minute of September with precision. The symposia, the exhibitions, the performances. But the most important moment you don't programme. Breakfast conversation. Midnight kitchen. Lobby encounter that becomes a three-year collaboration. Not happening — scattered across fifty hotels.
MOVEMENT IV
Breakfast table, seats twelve. Robotics PhD from ETH Zurich. Seventeen-year-old from Graz. Brazilian street artist, volunteer. Prix Ars jury member puts phone down. That table doesn't exist in any hotel. That conversation doesn't happen in your current infrastructure.
MOVEMENT V
Jury member finishes judging at 9 PM. Takes taxi twenty minutes to a hotel by Danube. Eats alone. Next morning, table for one. Never knows the awarded artist was three blocks away, eating alone at the same time. Two realities. One signature away from collision.
MOVEMENT VI
Not metaphor. Architecture. Oldenburg third places. Granovetter weak ties. Ars has exhibited on all of this: GEMeinsam, Kyrielle, Social Play. Given ideas a stage. Now give them a building.
MOVEMENT VII
The Basecamp. Ninety beds. One kitchen. One lounge. Walking distance from venues. Twenty-eight euros per night. Permanent Ars installation in lobby, curated year after year. Flow Zone satellite venue. Not five days. Three hundred and sixty-five.
MOVEMENT VIII
Where do Prix winners sleep? Not the famous ones — the emerging ones. Twenty-four-year-old from Medellín. Researcher from Dakar. Seoul students on scholarship. Sleep in village. Cook with Futurelab scientist. Walk to venue five minutes. Belong somewhere.
MOVEMENT IX
Your mission: art, technology, and society intersect. Our building: loneliness crisis, spatial architecture, creative community since 1979. Not two projects. One project. Two addresses.
MOVEMENT X
Fifteen thousand euros for three years. Twenty-five beds during festival week at twenty-eight euros per night. Transferable vouchers. Installation space twelve months. Priority booking. SDG 3, 11, 17. Tax-deductible. Full refund if hostel doesn't open. Zero risk. Zero complexity.
MOVEMENT XI
Not asking for a decision. Asking for a conversation. Next step: one-page letter of intent. No money. No legal obligation. No board approval necessary. When Sparkasse sees "Ars Electronica" on the commitment sheet, they move. Doors open that were closed.
MOVEMENT XII
Last exhibition closes. Stages go dark. One hundred and twenty-two thousand go home. Festival ends every September 15. Village doesn't. Runs forever. Nineteen seventy-nine, you built the festival. Twenty twenty-seven, build the village.
