## The Big Idea

Linz Hostel transforms a section of TabakFabrik Linz — one of Europe's most ambitious industrial-to-creative repurposing projects — into a hostel that doesn't just house travelers, it activates them.

The core concept: **Teachcation**. Instead of sightseeing as passive consumption, guests at Linz Hostel become temporary makers. A welder teaches metalwork on Tuesday. A ceramicist runs a Wednesday workshop. A digital artist opens their studio Saturday morning. Guests leave with something they made with their own hands — and a story that can't be Instagrammed away.

The TabakFabrik context is everything. It's not a trend — it's a 1930s building that once employed 3,000 workers making cigarettes and now houses 140+ creative businesses. Linz Hostel is the missing hospitality layer in an already-functioning creative ecosystem.

## Business Model

**Revenue Streams:**
1. Hostel beds — €28–85/night depending on room type
2. Maker workshop revenue share (30% of ticket price)
3. Coworking day passes — €20/day, €199/month
4. Event space rental — €500–2,500/booking
5. Teachcation packages — €350–900/week all-in

**3-Scenario Financial Model (v4.2.3):**
- Conservative: 60% occupancy → break-even at month 18
- Base: 72% occupancy → profitable from month 12
- Optimistic: 82% occupancy + Teachcation premium → €180K EBITDA Year 2

**Funding Target: €400K**
- €100K equity
- €220K Sparkasse Linz loan
- €80K grants (Tourism Austria + ERDF regional development)
